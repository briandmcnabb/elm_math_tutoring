a bit
about
me...

I live in Brooklyn with my husband, Brian, and our dog, Zeke. I've been teaching math for the last 4 years, and, at the risk of exposing my nerdiness, I'll admit that I've been loving it. There's always a new way to think about a problem, and always a better way of teaching it. The joy comes in finding what works best for different students. Tutoring lets me focus on this.


To be honest, math wasn't always my favorite. I wasn't one of those kids who solved equations in my sleep, and I haven't always thought that mathematical proofs were the bee's knees. Still, I chose to get my degree in math. Why? Unlike most kids, I was lucky enough to have a calculus teacher who managed to make math seem fun. Now, 8 years later, I'm in the business of doing the same for others. It's not about rote memorization or constant drilling; It's about tearing down mental barriers and finding new ways to think about problems.
